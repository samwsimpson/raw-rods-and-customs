Executive Summary

Texas Vehicle Assembly Advantage

Texas Administrative Code Title 43, Part 10 allows for "assembled vehicles" to be registered via:

  • VTR-68-A Form: Assembled Vehicle Statement
  • Safety Inspection: Required before registration
  • No Emissions Test: Kit cars exempt in most Texas counties

Legal Protection: All vehicles sold with "For Off-Road/Track Use" disclaimer until customer completes state inspection.

Risk Management & Mitigation

⚠️ Key Risks

Supply Chain Disruption

Risk: Factory Five or Cleveland PAP delays

Mitigation: Maintain 2-unit buffer inventory; develop backup supplier relationships (Blueprint Engines, Ford Performance)

Liability Exposure

Risk: Customer accident attributed to assembly defects

Mitigation: $2M liability insurance; comprehensive pre-delivery inspection checklist; 30-day mechanical warranty; all sales include track-use waiver

Market Demand Variability

Risk: Economic downturn reduces luxury purchases

Mitigation: Diversify with rolling chassis service (lower price point); build 6-month cash reserve by Year 3

Regulatory Changes

Risk: Texas tightens kit car regulations

Mitigation: Active membership in SEMA; legal counsel on retainer; adaptable business model for compliance

✓ Warranty & Quality Assurance

30-Day "Texas Shake-Down" Warranty

Coverage: All mechanical assembly defects (leaks, loose components, wiring shorts, brake system issues)

Process: Customer drives 500 miles; returns for complimentary inspection and adjustment

Drivetrain Pass-Through Warranty

Coverage: Cleveland PAP's 12-month/12,000-mile powertrain warranty transfers directly to customer

Advantage: Customer has direct relationship with engine supplier for any powertrain issues

Pre-Delivery Quality Checklist

  • 100-point mechanical inspection
  • Dynamometer testing (if equipped)
  • Road test (20+ miles)
  • Fluid level verification
  • Torque spec verification on all critical fasteners
